Paddle Boarding Lessons and Excursions

Paddling has been documented for centuries across the globe. Modern paddle boarding, commonly referred to as Stand Up Paddling (SUP) originated in Hawaii as a way to parade the local kings around the island.
Paddle boarding has become a popular way to exercise and leisurely 'hover' atop the water giving a new perspective to exploring the waterways
Paddling is extremely fun and is a great way to exercise, burning 300 - 1000 calories per hour

Fitness Phoenix is equipped to support groups of up to 5 paddlers.
Tours last from 1 to 2 hours located in the local metro Atlanta area waterways, frequenting Fort Yargo State Park.
Local lakes include but are not limited to:

  • Fort Yargo *parking fees may apply
  • Tribble Mill
  • Lake Lanier *parking fees may apply
  • Keith Bridge Park
  • Lake Alatoona
  • Morgan Falls Overlook Park
  • Stone Mountain *parking fees may apply
Equipment will be provided to include the board, life jacket, board leash, and of course a paddle.
NOTE: Some boards are equipped to convert into a kayak with an optional seat and extended paddle head if interested.
* Restrictions apply.
**Boarders are limited to a weight limit of 200lbs.

Paddle boarding is a family adventure and I welcome your children to come along on the adventure.
In order to paddle, accompanying children

  • should be able to swim without a flotation device
  • are preferred to be at least 12 years of age. Exceptions may be permitted based on skill.
